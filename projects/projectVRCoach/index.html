Do the smiles and nods of a virtual coach impact the effectiveness of VR therapy?

Automated virtual VR therapy holds potential for expanding access to mental health treatments, and virtual coaches form a crucial element of automated therapy in delivering psychological support. Our study delves into whether the positive non-verbal behaviors exhibited by a virtual coach—such as warm facial expressions and affirmative nods—can amplify patient engagement in VR therapy.

In our experiment, we engaged 120 individuals with fear of heights and employed a two-by-two factorial, between-groups, randomized design. Participants interacted with a virtual coach whose non-verbal cues varied, featuring combinations of warmth in facial expressions and head nods. Results suggest positive emotional attributes on a VR coach could enhance people’s therapy engagement and belief in the treatment. In the development of automated VR therapies there is likely to be therapeutic value in detailed consideration of the animations of virtual coaches.
