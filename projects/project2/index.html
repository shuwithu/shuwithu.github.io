• VR Turing Test

    digital humanity

  • Topic
    VR Game * Social pyschological experiment

  • Skill
    VR Development with Unity 3D & C#; empirical research

Virtual reality (VR) technology is actively employed in modern social psychological research, aiming to understand human attitudes and behaviours in the virtual world. The digital agency (either computer-controlled agent or human-controlled avatar) manipulation has been applied to trigger different user reactions. However, research insights on the influence of the agency type on the perceived presence (sense of being in the virtual world) and social interaction decision making has been limited. The current study investigated the effects of different perceived agency on the presence level from three sub-scales measurement in spatial presence, involvement and experienced realism. It also tested the relationship between agency type and in-game altruism (helping behaviour with a cost to the self). 32 university students with various gaming experience levels played a VR adventure game with either the perceived human-avatar or computer-agent.

The findings showed that people who perceived playing with human-avatars had higher presence level from the spatial presence and involvement relative to the computer-controlled agent group. No significant association was found between the agency perception and in-game altruism. Overall, the study enhanced current understanding about user experience and behaviour towards different agency in the virtual world.
