• 100 Days in Kumamoto

    data visualisation

  • Topic
    Social Network Analysis

  • Skill
    C#; Unity3D; Twitter RESET API

"Natural disasters may reflect a society’s image through social media."
This project was part of my summer research in Tokyo Tech about information visualization and social network analysis. To present the visualization topic of Kumamoto earthquake, a view (breakthrough point) of twitter sustained data analysis was chosen to further understand the emerging role of social media in disasters.

// The Challenge

With the chosen dataset of Twitter tweets, one of the challenges is to extract the relative posts of Kumamoto earthquake from the massive data in the 100 days. Furthermore, the project is also hoped to examine an effective way to present information aiming to enhance one’s sense of involvement.

// The Solution

  • Concept Design
    Define my user: Rather than reader or recipients of information, users of the visualization implementation would be participants.
    Define the experience: User is expected to explore the information from a “first person” perspective experience in the immersive environment.
  • Data Extraction Solution: Due to the scrape range limitation of current twitter RSET API, an independent Python program was developed for tracking history data. A total number of 9,724 tweets were extracted from 2016.4.14 to 2016.7.22 during the 100 days of the Kumamoto earthquake.
  • Interactive Environment Design Solution: A 100 days of Kumomato spiral stairs was designed as the basic model which the user will go through to explore the tweet information. A series of event milestones are extracted and set on the two sides of the stair, one is to represent the disasters and its side-effect, the other is for the strategies and measures on how the society dealt with it. User will join the experience as a first-person player to explore those tweets data by picking up the twitter bird game object.
